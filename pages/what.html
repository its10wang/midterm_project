ARTISTS

  • ZION.T

    Zion.T is the stage alias of Kim Hae-sol, a popular South Korean singer and songwriter recognized for his smooth vocal delivery and genre-sampling R&B. Kim studied music briefly at the college level, but decided that it wasn't for him, determined to record on his own. He released his first song, "Click Me," in the spring of 2011, and had his first chart appearance with the stand-alone single "Two Melodies" (featuring Crush) in 2013. Zion.T's debut album, a set of off-kilter R&B titled Red Light, arrived later that year via Amoeba Culture and featured guest rappers such as Beenzeno and Gaeko.

  • MURDA BEATZ

    Canadian hip-hop record producer Murda Beatz is a veteran of the industry, having produced tracks for top names like Drake, Travis Scott, and Migos. Considered the Canadian king of trap music, Murda Beatz creates iconic beats that have been celebrated by the hip-hop scene for years.

  • SEN MORIMOTO

    Kyoto born and Chicago based, this jazz-rapping multi-instrumentalist puts a dynamic twist on all of his music-making. His saxophone playing adds unique layer of depth to his music, sure to make you feel dreamy and introspective throughout any live performance he delivers.

  • KEITH APE

    "South Korea's first real rap star," (i-D) Keith Ape broke down barriers with his hit single, "It G Ma," which has racked up over 50M views as the first Asian rap record to achieve mainstream success. Since his initial breakthrough, Keith Ape has ascended the ranks to hip-hop royalty and continues to facilitate groundbreaking intercultural collaborations.

  • KOHH

    Born April 22nd 1990 in the town of Ouji in Kita Ward Tokyo City. Composing lyrics that express the realistic and often rough side of life in a raw and powerful form, he has an uncategorized style that is free of musical genres gaining immense support of mainly a young audience domestically as well as internationally. On top of his music endeavours, he is actively involved as a fashion model on the runway, such as providing artworks that he had a core involvement in at various exhibitions, proving himself to be an artist that goes beyond that of a rapper and musician.

  • DIABLO

    Diablo has been taking over the production game in South Florida, and his roster of rappers he’s worked with more than proves it. Ski Mask, Smokepurpp, Lil Pump are just a few to name, but Diablo is quickly proving himself to rise to the top of hip-hop producers and DJing all over beloved venues like Webster Hall in the U.S.

  • JOJI

    Osaka, Japan-born singer and producer Joji is a multi-talented visionary, with a cult following from every corner of the internet. Joji defies convention -- his songs characterized by down tempo, melancholic themes and soulful vocals, layered over lush grimy textures, blending elements of trap, folk, electronic, and R&B.   • NIKI

    At only 19-years-old, Indonesian R&B artist NIKI has already achieved global recognition from the four corners of her bedroom as “the internet’s favorite R&B princess” (Noisey). NIKI’s breakout single “See U Never” quickly amassed over 2M streams, garnering praise from media powerhouse Vice, among others. Her follow-up releases, “I Like U” and “Chilly” achieved similar success, further solidifying her rise alongside “new-school goddesses” into the R&B pantheon (Vice).

  • DUMBFOUNDEAD

    From LA battle rapper to one of the most well-known Asian rappers in the U.S., Dumbfoundead is well-loved by the rap scene for his witty and conscious lyrics. His music is playful, but consistently holds an undercurrent of political truth, which has brought him collaborations like Anderson Paak. and Jay Park.
